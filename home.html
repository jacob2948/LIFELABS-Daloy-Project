Water Relevance

Water is the most important resources on Earth. Every living thing from people to plants and animals—depends on clean water to survive. However, our water sources are now in danger because of pollution caused by human activities. Factories, farms, and even households produce waste that often ends up in rivers, lakes, and oceans. The group research and proposal, The Water Project, aims to spread awareness about water pollution, how it affects our environment, and what we can do to protect and restore clean water for everyone.

It is really sad to see how much trash is around us. Everywhere we look, there are piles of waste that people did not throw away properly. If we keep ignoring this problem, our planet will suffer even more. We should all do our part to take care of the environment, because even small actions like throwing garbage in the right place can make a big difference

THEN

About 20 years ago (early 2000s), water pollution in the Philippines was already a severe crisis, driven mainly by untreated sewage, industrial waste, and agricultural runoff. Major rivers like the Pasig and Laguna de Bay were considered biologically “dead” in sections, and contamination posed serious health risks, especially to urban poor communities.

NOW

In the present day, the Philippines has made progress in flood control measures and strengthened laws against deforestation. However, environmental pollution remains a critical issue. Every minute, dozens of pieces of plastic enter waterways, and each day this accumulation worsens. The impact is especially visible during floods, when garbage and plastic waste float through communities, compounding the damage and highlighting the urgent need for stronger waste management solutions.

Why the Water Pollution

Pollution hurts not just our environment but all of us, when the air is polluted, and water turns dirty, animals and humans suffering from environmental pollution.

Pollution also affects both Agriculture and Fishery Arts. When the air, soil, and water become polluted, it becomes harder for farmers to grow healthy crops and raise animals. Dirty water and harmful chemicals can damage plants and make the soil less fertile. In Fishery Arts, pollution harms fish and other sea animals because waste and plastics in rivers and oceans make the water unsafe. Fishermen lose their source of food and income, and people can get sick from eating contaminated seafood.

The Philippines has been greatly affected by environmental pollution due to active human activities that’s greatly contaminating our waters, land, and even air.
